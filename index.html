About Me

I am a final-year Ph.D. candidate at Department of Computer Science and Engineering, The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK). I am fortunate to be supervised by Prof. Wei Meng. I received my bachelor's degree from University of Chinese Academy of Sciences in 2019.

I am generally interested in software security and software engineering. I develop scalable static analysis and dynamic testing approaches for automated vulnerability detection. My research so far has found hundreds of new bugs in foundational system software, e.g., Linux kernel and GitHub, and has received recognition with awards from academia and industry.

In my security research journey, I have fruitful research experience working with Prof. Chao Zhang from Tsinghua University, Prof. Kangjie Lu from University of Minnesota, and Prof. Kai Chen from Chinese Academy of Sciences.
